Step 1: Critical Response (0-5 minutes)
Immediate safety and containment actions
  • 1
    Ensure Patient Safety: Immediately move patients to safe area if equipment poses risk
  • 2
    Power Down Equipment: If safe to do so, power down malfunctioning equipment
  • 3
    Secure Area: Prevent unauthorized access to failed equipment
  • 4
    Call Emergency Line: Contact MedAsset Partners at (480) 840-7352
  • 5
    Activate Backup Systems: Switch to backup equipment if available
🔍
Step 2: Damage Assessment (5-15 minutes)
Evaluate scope and impact of failure
  • 1
    Document Symptoms: Record error messages, unusual sounds, visual indicators
  • 2
    Check Power Sources: Verify electrical connections and power supply status
  • 3
    Test Related Systems: Check if failure has affected connected equipment
  • 4
    Estimate Impact: Determine number of procedures/patients affected
  • 5
    Photo Documentation: Take photos of error screens and visible damage
⚡
Step 3: Immediate Actions (15-30 minutes)
Stabilize operations and prepare for repair
  • 1
    Reschedule Appointments: Contact affected patients and reschedule procedures
  • 2
    Prepare Work Area: Clear access to equipment for technician arrival
  • 3
    Gather Information: Collect equipment manuals, service history, warranty info
  • 4
    Alternative Arrangements: Contact partner facilities for emergency capacity
  • 5
    Staff Briefing: Inform all staff of situation and adjusted procedures
📢
Step 4: Communication Protocol (Ongoing)
Keep stakeholders informed throughout process
  • 1
    Notify Administration: Alert facility management and medical directors
  • 2
    Update Patients: Communicate delays and alternative arrangements
  • 3
    Insurance Contact: Notify insurance providers if applicable
  • 4
    Vendor Coordination: Maintain regular contact with MedAsset Partners technicians
  • 5
    Progress Updates: Provide hourly updates to key stakeholders
🔄
Step 5: Recovery & Follow-up (Post-Resolution)
Restore operations and prevent future incidents
  • 1
    System Testing: Thoroughly test repaired equipment before resuming operations
  • 2
    Staff Training: Brief staff on any system changes or new procedures
  • 3
    Documentation: Complete incident report with timeline and lessons learned
  • 4
    Schedule Makeup: Reschedule displaced appointments at earliest opportunity
  • 5
    Prevention Review: Analyze root cause and implement preventive measures
  • 6
    Plan Update: Revise emergency response plan based on experience
