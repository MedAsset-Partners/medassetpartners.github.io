The Cost of Equipment Downtime

When medical equipment fails unexpectedly, the impact extends far beyond repair costs:

  • Lost revenue from canceled appointments
  • Patient dissatisfaction and potential loss
  • Emergency repair costs and rush fees
  • Staff downtime and reduced productivity

Components of Effective Preventive Maintenance

A comprehensive preventive maintenance program includes:

  • Regular Inspections: Scheduled visual and performance checks
  • Calibration Services: Ensuring accuracy and precision
  • Parts Replacement: Proactive replacement of wear items
  • Performance Testing: Verifying equipment meets specifications
  • Documentation: Detailed records for compliance and tracking

Creating Your Maintenance Schedule

Effective scheduling considers multiple factors:

  • Manufacturer recommendations and intervals
  • Equipment usage patterns and intensity
  • Environmental factors and operating conditions
  • Historical performance and failure patterns

ROI of Preventive Maintenance

Studies show that every dollar spent on preventive maintenance saves $3-5 in avoided breakdowns and extended equipment life. Benefits include:

  • Extended equipment lifespan by 20-40%
  • Reduced emergency repair costs
  • Improved equipment reliability and performance
  • Better compliance with regulatory requirements
